# A Comprehensive Review of Techniques for Documenting Artificial Intelligence

## Author
- Florian Königstorfer

## Publication
- **Type:** Research Paper (Structured Literature Review)
- **Year:** 2024
- **Keywords:** AI Documentation, AI Development, AI Governance

## Summary
This paper presents a structured literature review of 38 papers from top journals and conferences in the fields of medicine, information systems, and fair/accountable/transparent AI. The review examines the extent to which current documentation guidelines can meet the documentation requirements for AI applications as defined by regulatory bodies (e.g., the EU AI Act) and industry practitioners. The findings reveal that contemporary documentation methods are fragmented — often focusing on specific aspects like training data or model development, or limited to particular industries. The paper identifies three key avenues for future research: (1) creating comprehensive guidelines that satisfy all documentation requirements, (2) addressing the documentation challenges of evolving AI technologies such as frequently retrained models, pre-trained foundation models, and generative AI, and (3) evaluating the effectiveness of automated documentation tools, including LLMs.

The paper covers:
1. Introduction and motivation

3. Methodology (structured literature review per Webster and Watson)
4. Results organized by documentation requirement:
   - Documenting training data
   - Documenting the application domain
   - Documenting design decisions
5. Discussion: Gaps and future research directions
6. Conclusion and limitations
